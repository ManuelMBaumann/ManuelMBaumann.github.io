Manuel Baumann, PhD

I am an R&D research engineer for the numerical simulation of electromagnetics at Dassault Systèmes. Before joining the industry, I was a researcher at Philips Research in Hamburg and a PostDoc in the group Simulation of Energy Systems at the Max Planck Institute in Magdeburg. In 2018, I graduated with a PhD in Applied Mathematics from Delft University of Technology. My research focus is numerical linear algebra, computational imaging and model-order reduction.

Research interests

From my point of view, the field Numerical Analysis is best described as the connecting scientific discipline between theoretical considerations of physical phenomena which arise in engineering science and their numerical solution on state-of-the-art hardware. In more detail, my scientific interest includes mostly partial differential equations and their fast numerical solution after discretization. This typically leads to large sparse linear systems of equations. The fast numerical solution of large-scale linear systems with iterative methods is a key problem in numerical linear algebra. During the last years, the following fields have been the focus of my scientific interest:

  • Krylov subspace methods for shifted linear systems
    [SISC paper], [JOCS paper], [iccs17], [APNUM paper & applet]

  • Preconditioning techniques and elastic wave equation
    [CompGeo paper], [EAGE16 proceedings]

  • Model-order reduction and PDE-constrained optimization
    [SISC paper], [VM60 chapter], [MSc thesis]

  • High performance computing with MPI and CUDA
    [BSc thesis]
