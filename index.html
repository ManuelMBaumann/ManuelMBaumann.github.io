Research interests

From my point of view, the field Numerical Analysis is best described as the connecting scientific discipline between theoretical considerations of physical phenomena which arise in engineering science (Modeling) and their numerical solution with the computer (Computer Science). In more detail, my scientific interest includes mostly partial differential equations and their fast numerical solution after discretization. This leads, typically, to large linear systems of equations. The fast numerical solution of large linear systems with iterative solvers is a key problem in numerical linear algebra. During my studies and since the beginning of my PhD, the following fields have been the focus of my scientific interest:

  • Numerical Linear Algebra, and preconditioning for Krylov subspace methods in particular
  • Numerical methods for partial differential equations
  • High performance computing with MPI and CUDA
  • Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)
  • Model order reduction for linear and nonlinear dynamical systems
  • PDE-constrained optimization
